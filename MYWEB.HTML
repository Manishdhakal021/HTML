After the Feud, Kendrick Lamar Unleashes New Album ‘GNX

Kendrick Lamar has just dropped his highly anticipated new album, “GNX,” and the hip-hop world is already buzzing! The album brings together a mix of hard-hitting bangers, smooth West Coast vibes, and thought-provoking lyrics that only Kendrick can deliver. “GNX” features collaborations with top artists such as SZA, Roddy Ricch, and other emerging talents, giving each track a fresh and dynamic sound. Fans are raving about standout songs, unique production, and Kendrick’s signature storytelling, making it clear that this release is another milestone in his career. From the opening track to the last, “GNX” captures the energy, creativity, and culture of modern hip-hop, quickly becoming the talk of the streets and streaming charts everywhere. Whether you’re a longtime fan or new to his music, this album is a must-listen and is already making waves across social media and playlists worldwide.

TRACKLIST OF THE ALBUM "GNX"

1. Wacced Out Murals

Produced by Sounwave, Jack Antonoff, Craig Balmoris, Dahi, Frano, Tyler Reese, M‑Tech, and Tim Maxey. This opening track sets the tone with layered production and contributions from Mexican singer Deyra Barrera (vocals) as Kendrick reflects on his career and recent events

2. Squabble Up

Produced by Sounwave, Jack Antonoff, Scott Bridgeway, M‑Tech, and Kendrick himself. Released as a lead single, it runs about 2:37, showing Kendrick’s West Coast energy with a punchy beat and no featured rappers.

3.Luther (feat. SZA)

Produced by Sounwave, Jack Antonoff, Kamasi Washington, Rose Lilah, M‑Tech, and Bridgeway. Features SZA with additional vocals from Sam Dew; a standout song that blends Kendrick’s rap with melodic R&B influences from the duo

4. Man at the Garden

Produced by Sounwave, Jack Antonoff, Craig Balmoris, Tyler Reese, and M‑Tech. A 3:53 track built on lush, moody synths that finds Kendrick asserting his worth and legacy in a self‑reflective way.

5. Hey Now

Produced by Jack Antonoff, Mustard, and Sounwave with vocals from Dody6 and background from Sam Dew. The production combines West Coast grooves and classic elements while Kendrick and guest vocals interplay

6. Reincarnated

Produced by Sounwave, Jack Antonoff, Kendrick Lamar, M‑Tech, and Noah Ehler. A longer cut (around 4:35) that samples Tupac and melds historical influence with Kendrick’s own narrative voice.

7. TV OFF

Produced by Sounwave, Jack Antonoff, Mustard, Kamasi Washington, and Sean Momberger. Features additional vocals from Lefty Gunplay; an energetic track with layered instrumentation that bridges hip‑hop and live‑inspired production.

8. Dodger Blue

Produced by Sounwave, Jack Antonoff, Tane Runo, Tim Maxey, and Terrace Martin. At 2:11, it features Wallie the Sensei, Siete7x, and Roddy Ricch (plus additional vocals from INK), giving off classic G‑funk vibes and a tribute to LA culture.

9. Peekaboo (feat. AzChike)

Produced by Bridgeway, Sean Momberger, and Sounwave, this 2:35 track features AzChike with a catchy bounce and memorable hook that has already seen radio play as a single.

10. Heart pt.6

Produced by Sounwave, Jack Antonoff, M‑Tech, and Juju the Fool. Part of Kendrick’s long‑running The Heart series, this song samples SWV and features subtle background vocals from Sam Dew, blending introspection with Kendrick’s signature rhymes

11.GNX (feat. Hitta J3, Peysoh & Young Threat)

Produced by Sounwave, Jack Antonoff, Kenny & Billy, Rascal, and Tim Maxey. This title track brings together underground voices Hitta J3, Peysoh, and Young Threat over a hard‑hitting beat that reflects the album’s high‑energy themes

12. Gloria (feat. SZA)

Produced by Sounwave, Jack Antonoff, and Deats, this closing track runs about 4:47 and reunites Kendrick with SZA for a smoother, emotionally charged finale that blends hip‑hop and R&B textures.

Grammy Nominations 2025

leads all 2025 Grammy nominees with 11 nods, with Charli XCX, Billie Eilish, Kendrick Lamar & Post Malone securing seven nominations.

When nominations for the 67th annual Grammy Awards were announced Friday morning (Nov. 8), Beyoncé became the most-nominated artist in the show’s history — breaking out of a tie with her husband, Jay-Z.Beyoncé picks up 11 new nominations, the most for any artist going into the 2025 Grammy ceremony. Next in line are Charli XCX, Billie Eilish, Kendrick Lamar and Post Malone with seven nods apiece, followed by Sabrina Carpenter, Chappell Roan and Taylor Swift with six each. Bey now has 99 total nominations (and is also the winningest artist in Grammy history with 32 trophies). The new nominations are spread across multiple Grammy fields, thanks to her genre-bending 2024 album Cowboy Carter, which earns three general nods (album of the year, plus record and song of the year for lead single “Texas Hold ‘Em”) as well as four nominations in country categories, two in pop, one in rap, and even one in Americana. Carpenter and Roan join the elite club of artists who have been nominated in all of the Big Four categories, with both vying for best new artist plus album of the year (Short n’ Sweet and The Rise and Fall of a Midwest Princess, respectively), record of the year (Carpenter’s “Espresso” and Roan’s “Good Luck, Babe!”) and song of the year (Carpenter’s “Please Please Please” vs. “Good Luck, Babe!”).
